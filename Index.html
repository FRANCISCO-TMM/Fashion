The House

Built on the fitting, not the trend

The Prestige Couture was founded in 1992 around a single rule: a garment is drafted on a person, not a mannequin. The Paris cutting room sits beside a hand-embroidery workshop trained in Lunéville needlework and a finishing studio where every hem is closed by hand.

Each client's paper pattern is logged into the house archive and kept indefinitely. A coat first cut in 1998 can be remade today against its original pattern, stitch for stitch — which is also why our records read more like a ledger than a lookbook.
